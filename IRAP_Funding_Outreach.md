# IRAP Funding Outreach Guide - AddManuChain

The National Research Council of Canada Industrial Research Assistance Program (NRC IRAP) provides advice, connections, and funding to help Canadian small and medium-sized businesses increase their innovation capacity and take ideas to market. 

To secure funding, you do not just "apply"â€”you must first build a relationship with an **Industrial Technology Advisor (ITA)** who will guide you to the right programs and help you build a project proposal.

## Steps to Contact IRAP

### 1. Verify Basic Eligibility
Before reaching out, ensure AddManuChain and your company meet the baseline criteria:
*   Be a registered, for-profit small or medium-sized enterprise (SME) in Canada.
*   Have fewer than 500 full-time equivalent employees.
*   Plan to pursue growth and profit by developing and commercializing innovative, technology-driven new or improved products, services, or processes in Canada.

### 2. Prepare Your "Elevator Pitch" Materials
Be ready to clearly communicate your startup's status. Have the following prepared:
*   A concise description of the **AddManuChain** project (what it is, the problem it solves).
*   Your target market and insights from your **Lab2Market customer discovery** (e.g., your 50+ interviews with offshore energy, aerospace, and marine gatekeepers).
*   Your business model and commercialization plan.
*   The technical challenges or R&D remaining that you need funding to accomplish.

### 3. Contact IRAP to Be Assigned an ITA
You have two main ways to initiate contact:
*   **Direct Phone Call (Fastest):** Call the IRAP toll-free number at **1-877-994-4727**. A representative will ask you a few questions about your business to connect you with the most appropriate local ITA (likely in the Atlantic region or wherever your company is based).
*   **Through Existing Connections:** Since you are in Lab2Market, ask your mentors (e.g., Dwayne Hopkins, Carol, or the L2M regional directors) if they have a direct contact or can make a warm introduction to an ITA who covers ocean tech or additive manufacturing.

### 4. The Initial ITA Meeting
Once connected, the ITA will likely ask for an introductory call or meeting. 
*   **Goal:** The ITA wants to understand your project's technical merit, market potential, and your team's capability.
*   Treat this meeting like a pitch, but be transparent about where you need help (IRAP funds technical development/innovation, not just general marketing or operations).
*   **Outcome:** If the ITA sees a fit, they will invite you to submit a funding proposal. **The ITA acts as your advocate** inside IRAP.

### 5. Develop the Project Proposal
Work closely with your ITA to develop a detailed project proposal, which outlines the specific R&D tasks, timelines, costs, and expected outcomes that IRAP will co-fund.
