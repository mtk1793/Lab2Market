## Main Questions (5)

---

### Q1: The Hidden Cost Drivers in Large-Scale Shipyard Operations

> **"Over your 30 years in shipbuilding — from Irving to Davie to Ontario Shipyards — you've managed programs ranging from $100M to $1B+. In your experience, what are the true cost drivers that shock stakeholders? Is it materials, labor, rework, schedule delays, or something else entirely?"**

**Probes:**
- When you took over as CEO at Davie post-receivership, what was the first area where you found waste or inefficiency? (Procurement? Schedule? Workforce?)
- In a fixed-price or cost-plus government contract, what typically causes unexpected cost escalation? Is it scope creep, supplier delays, design changes, or coordination overhead?
- Of the $8M in annual overhead savings you achieved at Davie, what was the single biggest contributor? (Process automation? Headcount? Procurement discipline?)
- How much of a shipyard's cost variance would you estimate comes from **supplier/procurement risk** vs. internal operations?

**Why this matters:** Quantifies where waste actually occurs. If coordination/visibility is the hidden cost driver, there's a hypothesis to validate about digital orchestration ROI.

---

### Q2: Managing Supplier Complexity at Scale — The Visibility Problem

> **"At Ontario Shipyards, you managed a portfolio of suppliers ranging from $100K to $20M agreements. In a typical multi-year shipbuilding program with hundreds of suppliers, how do you currently track whether a critical supplier is at risk of delay, quality failure, or cost overrun?"**

**Probes:**
- Walk me through your current process: How do you know if Supplier A is going to be late with a critical component 4 weeks from now?
- Is this visibility data-driven (real-time dashboards), relationship-based (frequent calls), or reactive (problems surface when they're already causing delays)?
- How much time do you and your team spend firefighting supplier issues vs. proactively identifying and preventing them?
- What percentage of your supplier base is "low-risk" (proven, predictable) vs. "high-risk" (new, variable, geographically distant)?
- If you could see supplier risk 2–4 weeks earlier, what would you do with that signal? (Shift orders? Activate backup suppliers? Accelerate expediting?)

**Why this matters:** Explores whether **early visibility into supplier/schedule risk** is a meaningful pain point and whether addressable with digital tools.

---

### Q3: Procurement & Tendering — Where Does Time Get Lost?

> **"You've led procurement teams through federal/provincial tendering and managed vendor selection processes. Where do you lose the most time in procurement — in defining specs, comparing bids, contract review, or something else? And what's the cost of that delay?"**

**Probes:**
- Typical timeline: From RFQ issuance to purchase order for a non-trivial component — how long does it take today? 3 months? 6 months?
- Where is the bottleneck? Are you reviewing 50+ competitive bids manually? Are engineering specs slow to finalize? Are contract terms taking weeks to negotiate?
- If a critical supplier's quote comes in higher than expected, how long does it take to identify and qualify an alternative?
- How often do you find yourself expediting or paying premium freight/labor to recover from a procurement delay that could have been prevented?
- If you could compress your procurement cycle by 2–4 weeks without sacrificing quality, what would that unlock? (Better cost, schedule buffer, vendor choice?)

**Why this matters:** Tests whether **procurement cycle time** is a real pain point and whether digital tools (automated bid comparison, template contracting, etc.) would have material ROI.

---

### Q4: Workforce Scaling & Union Dynamics — Hidden Operational Costs

> **"You famously scaled Davie's workforce from 41 to 1,000+ in nine months post-receivership. That's an extraordinary feat. But what did that velocity cost in terms of operational friction, training gaps, safety incidents, or morale challenges? And how does union complexity amplify those costs?"**

**Probes:**
- In a 9-month scaling sprint, how do you maintain quality, safety, and morale? What breaks first?
- Walk me through a realistic scenario: You need 200 additional welders in 12 weeks. What's your process? (External hiring? Training pipeline? Contract labor?) What goes wrong?
- Union negotiations: You secured a 5-year agreement at Davie — was that primarily about wage/benefits, or did you negotiate flexibility in workforce deployment, cross-training, or operational changes?
- How much of your operational overhead is driven by compliance obligations, union rules, or training/certification requirements that could be streamlined?
- If you had perfect visibility into workforce readiness (skills inventory, certification status, availability), how would that change your ability to respond to program changes or delays?

**Why this matters:** Validates workforce coordination as a pain point and explores whether **digital workforce intelligence** (skills tracking, readiness planning) has ROI in union-heavy environments.

---

### Q5: Decision-Making Under Uncertainty — What Information Would Change Your Choices?

> **"You've navigated high-stakes environments: post-receivership turnaround, $1B contracts, government customers, union negotiations. When you're facing a critical decision — like whether to bring on a backup supplier, accelerate procurement, or adjust the schedule — what information do you *wish* you had that you don't have today?"**

**Probes:**
- Think of a recent decision at Horizon or Ontario Shipyards where you had to commit with incomplete information. What was missing?
- If you had **predictive visibility** into three areas — (a) program cost trajectory, (b) supplier performance risk, (c) schedule feasibility — which would have the highest impact on your decision quality?
- How often do you make a decision today and then find out 2–4 weeks later that the underlying assumption was wrong? What's the cost of that lag?
- What would "good" look like? A real-time dashboard? Weekly management reports? Alerts when risk thresholds are crossed?
- Would you pay for a tool/service that reduced uncertainty in any of these areas, or is this something you manage through relationships and experience?

**Why this matters:** Discovers whether **decision support/intelligence** is a real need and what form would be valuable (and worth paying for).
