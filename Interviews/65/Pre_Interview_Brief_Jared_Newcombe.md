## LIKELY PAIN POINTS TO VALIDATE

- **Supply Chain Complexity** — Managing hundreds of suppliers, procurement timelines, quality assurance, material delays across a multi-year program
- **Contract Risk & Cost Control** — Fixed-price or cost-plus contracts with tight margins; unpredictable change orders; scope creep
- **Workforce Coordination** — Union negotiations, workforce planning, training pipelines, safety compliance in high-stakes environments
- **Stakeholder Alignment** — Federal/provincial customers, unions, board, supply partners all pulling in different directions
- **Data & Visibility** — Lack of real-time insight into program health, supplier performance, cost trends, schedule risk
- **Decision Speed** — Time to act on emerging risks or opportunities

---

## HYPOTHESIS TO EXPLORE

**H1:** In large shipyard/maritime operations, the biggest cost drivers are not materials or labor costs themselves, but the **coordination overhead** and **rework** caused by poor visibility into supplier readiness, schedule risk, and program health.

**H2:** Digital orchestration or predictive intelligence at the commercial/operational intersection would have high ROI if it could:
- Flag supplier/schedule risk 2–4 weeks early (giving time to act)
- Reduce procurement cycle time by automating vendor comparison and contract review
- Provide early warning on cost trajectory vs. budget
