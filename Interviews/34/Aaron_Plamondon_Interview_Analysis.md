### 2. The Navy AM Failure ‚Äî "We've Never Been Successful"
Aaron admitted something remarkable ‚Äî **Irving and MAMCE invested in AM but never got a single part produced for the Navy.** Despite years of effort.

**Root Causes Identified:**
1. **Fear of risk** ‚Äî "There's lives at stake, it's super scary for someone to agree to that"
2. **No certification standard** ‚Äî "I don't think there IS a certification level"
3. **No testing infrastructure** ‚Äî Only ~2 companies in Canada do shock/vibration testing for defence
4. **No admiral's signature** ‚Äî "Until the Navy says 'go do this,' we're not going to get anywhere"
5. **Material uncertainty** ‚Äî "I don't think anyone has enough trust in the material to say in 5 years that's not going to break"

**üí° Implication:** This validates the **massive gap** AddManuChain is trying to fill. Even Canada's largest shipbuilder, with university partnerships and MAMCE's powder research, couldn't crack AM adoption. The barrier isn't technology ‚Äî it's governance, testing, and trust.
