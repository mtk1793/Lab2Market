### **1. You recently posted asking "We have CAD models, so why is digital inventory a new thing?" - what's the missing link that would make digital inventory actually valuable vs just CAD file management?**

**Why Critical:**
- **Direct challenge to our core value prop** (NH.9 - Digital inventory reduces lead time)

**What to Listen For:**
- Does he mention certification metadata, material specs, process parameters?

- Does he see value or dismiss the entire concept?

### **2. At Tronosjet and Renishaw, what's been the biggest barrier to getting customers to move from AM prototyping to production parts?**

**Why Critical:**
- Tests NH.2 (Obsolescence driver), NH.7 (Certification gatekeeper), H50 (Risk categorization)
- Validates whether our "risk categorization tool" (H50) is actually the MVP feature
- Aerospace context is our target market

**What to Listen For:**

### **3. When Tronosjet deals with obsolete aircraft parts, do you reverse engineer them or do OEMs provide digital files? What's the biggest bottleneck in that process?**

**Why Critical:**
- **Direct test of H52** (Reverse engineering integration)
- Tests NH.10 (OEM IP release blocker)

**What to Listen For:**
- Who owns the reverse engineering process - facility or customer?
- Is IP release the blocker, or is it scanning accuracy/certification approval?
- Would a marketplace of pre-scanned, qualification-ready parts be valuable?

### **4. For aerospace/medical manufacturers using your services, what percentage have their own certification/qualification expertise vs needing guidance on which standards apply?**
