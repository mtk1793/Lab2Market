# Interview Questions: Craig Dicken
**Position:** Materials Management Senior Principal, ExxonMobil Global Supply Chain  
**Focus Areas:** Procurement, supply chain operations, strategic sourcing, MRO supply chain, energy sector logistics

---

## Core Questions (5)

1. **Current State of Supply Chain Visibility**
   - In your experience managing large-scale supply chains (especially in energy), how much real-time visibility do you actually have into material flows, supplier performance, and inventory status?
   - What's currently lost or delayed in translation from warehouse/operational data to decisions that get made?

2. **Legacy Systems & Integration Friction**
   - When implementing new supply chain capabilities (procurement, planning, logistics), what typically breaks between your ERP systems and newer point solutions?
   - How much manual work exists because systems don't talk to each other seamlessly?

3. **Decision-Making & Speed-to-Action**
   - From the moment a supply chain problem is identified, how long does it typically take to understand it fully and act on it?
   - What information gaps slow down that cycle?

4. **Evaluating & Adopting New Supply Chain Tools**
   - What's your framework for vetting a new supply chain solution before company-wide commitment?
   - What would a platform need to prove to earn trust from both technical teams and executive leadership?

5. **Metrics That Matter Most**
   - Beyond cost savings, what metrics do you actually track to evaluate supply chain performance and improvement?
   - How do you differentiate between tools that optimize the existing process versus those that fundamentally change how teams work?

---

## Bonus Questions (2)

**Bonus 1:** If you had to redesign one phase of your supply chain (from sourcing through delivery), where would you focus and why?

**Bonus 2:** Looking 5 years ahead, what's the one supply chain capability that large energy companies need to build?
