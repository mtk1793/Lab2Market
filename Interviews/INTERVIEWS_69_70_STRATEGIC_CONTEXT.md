## JP HUDON'S LIKELY POSITION

### Operational Reality (Asset Integrity Manager)

**His World:**
- Manages large, complex equipment (copper smeliting furnaces, converters, casting wheels)
- High-consequence failures = major downtime + safety risk + equipment damage
- 180 people, $40M/year budget, unionized environment
- Cutting-edge on RCA and Lean implementation

**Most Likely Primary Pain (Hypothesis):**
- **Not cost**, because $40M budget is significant; he's not penny-counting
- **Not just downtime**, because he's proactive about RCA + prevention
- **Likely: "We can predict/prevent most failures, but knowledge retention is our bottleneck"**

**Why?** Key phrase in his profile: "Reversed org chart pyramid where leaders support their teams"
- This suggests he cares about PEOPLE/KNOWLEDGE as much as systems

### What This Interview Will Reveal

**If primary pain is KNOWLEDGE RETENTION:**
- How much expertise walks out when experienced technicians retire?
- How much of his maintenance strategy is based on undocumented tacit knowledge?
- Would he invest in capturing/systematizing that knowledge?
- Is there a gap between "best practice" RCA and "actual practice" at his sites?

**If primary pain is STANDARDIZATION:**
- Can different shifts/technicians execute the same maintenance process?
- Is there variation in quality/outcomes based on who does the work?
- Would he value a repeatable, documented maintenance procedure library?
