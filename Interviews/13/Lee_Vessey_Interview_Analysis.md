## üí° **THE TWO CRITICAL USE CASES**

### **USE CASE 1: SUPPLY CHAIN & DIGITAL INVENTORY (E-INVENTORY)**

#### **The Vision:**

> **Direct Quote:** "I will buy the 3D model and the license for the company at the same time. And then in my e-inventory, I've got two in my warehouse and I have the license and I have the model. So I've got an unlimited warehouse of electronic of the inventory of those parts."

**Current Process:**
- Buy physical parts ‚Üí Store in physical warehouse ‚Üí Limited stock

**Future E-Inventory Process:**
- Buy physical parts (2 units) + 3D CAD model + printing license
- Store physical parts in warehouse
- Store digital models in e-inventory
- **Result:** "Unlimited digital warehouse"

#### **Business Benefits:**

1. **Obsolescence Solution:**
   > "Maybe the part is obsolete. For low quantities, I don't need to set up a big production run for injection molding or for casting. I can get my engineers to do it without a 20,000 quantity production run."

2. **Sovereign Resilience (MILITARY CRITICAL):**
   > "Half of our parts, maybe 1/3 of our parts come from overseas. They come from Germany, United States, Norway, Europe, all over the place. If there is a crisis, whatever the crisis‚Äîtariffs, Suez Canal ship gets blocked‚Äîthe global supply chain is disrupted. My ability to fix the ship is immediately impacted unless I have the 3D models and the licenses in my e-inventory."

   **Crisis Scenarios:**
   - Tariffs (current global trade tensions)
   - Suez Canal blockages (2021 Ever Given precedent)
   - International conflicts disrupting shipping
   - Pandemic-style supply chain disruptions

   **Result:** "Gives me more sovereign resilience‚Äîreally important."

3. **Reduced Lead Times:**
   - No waiting on overseas shipping
   - No customs/tariff delays
   - Print on-demand in Canada

4. **Working Capital Optimization:**
   - Reduce physical inventory carrying costs
   - Digital models cost much less to "store"
   - Only print what's needed when needed

## üî¨ **RESEARCH: CIRCUIT BOARD ADDITIVE MANUFACTURING**

### **Answer to Lee's Question:**

**YES, this technology exists. Here are the key players:**

#### **1. Nano Dimension (Israel/USA) - LEADER**
- **Technology:** DragonFly LDM (Liquid Deposition Modeling)
- **Capability:** 3D print PCBs (printed circuit boards) with conductive inks
- **Process:** Layer-by-layer deposition of dielectric and conductive materials
- **Use Case:** Rapid prototyping, obsolescence replacement, low-volume production
- **Military Adoption:** US DoD using for obsolete electronics replacement
- **Website:** nano-di.com

#### **2. Voltera (Canada!) - DESKTOP PCB PRINTER**
- **Location:** Kitchener, Ontario (Canadian company!)
- **Technology:** V-One PCB printer
- **Capability:** Print circuit boards, solder paste dispensing, reflow
- **Use Case:** Prototyping, small batches, obsolescence
- **Price Point:** $5,000-$10,000 (accessible for Navy workshops)
- **Website:** voltera.io

#### **3. Optomec (USA) - AEROSOL JET**
- **Technology:** Aerosol Jet printing
- **Capability:** Print conformal electronics, flex circuits, 3D electronics
- **Military Use:** US Air Force using for aircraft electronics repair
- **Website:** optomec.com

#### **4. NCAB Group - REVERSE ENGINEERING SERVICE**
- **Capability:** Scan obsolete PCBs, reverse engineer, manufacture replacements
- **Process:** X-ray scanning ‚Üí Gerber file creation ‚Üí Manufacturing
- **Use Case:** Obsolete boards with no documentation
- **Not strictly AM:** Uses conventional PCB manufacturing after scanning

#### **5. BotFactory (USA) - SQUINK PCB PRINTER**
- **Technology:** Desktop electronics printer
- **Capability:** Print PCBs, surface mount assembly
- **Use Case:** Prototyping and low-volume production

### **RECOMMENDATION FOR LEE:**

**Short-Term (Prototyping & Obsolescence):**
1. **Voltera V-One** (Canadian company, $5-10K)
   - Desktop solution for workshop
   - Good for simple 2-4 layer boards
   - Rapid prototyping capability

3. **Reverse Engineering Service** (NCAB or similar)

**Limitations to Note:**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Component placement:** AM can print the board, but you still need to source/place ICs, resistors, capacitors

- ‚ö†Ô∏è **High-frequency boards:** Advanced RF boards still challenging for AM
- ‚úÖ **Best for:** Low-frequency logic, power, simple analog circuits
