### ‚úÖ **CERTIFICATION = PRIMARY BOTTLENECK (NOT TECHNICAL CAPABILITY)**

**Status:** **HIGHEST BARRIER VALIDATED** (More than business case!)

**Dr. Nasiri's Framework:**

**Why Certification is Hard:**

1. **"AM developed so fast that authorities couldn't catch up"** ‚Üê Best quote
2. **Regulatory bodies (DNV, ASTM, Lloyd's Register) developing standards but:**
   - "All recommendations are too general"
   - "Not specifically talking about certain types of components"

**What Must Be Certified:**

| Item | Complexity | Cost Impact |
|------|------------|-------------|
| **The Part** | Materials testing, structural validation | High |
| **The Operator** | Personnel qualification | Medium |
| **The Powder/Feedstock** | Batch consistency, traceability | High |
| **Processing Variables** | Machine calibration, temperature, speed | High |
| **The Labs** | Third-party validation facility accreditation | Very High |

**Dr. Nasiri's Quote:**
> "The whole process is **costly and time demanding**. This application is for the part where the application is structural."

**Current Process:**
1. AM facility produces part
2. Contact registry (DNV, Lloyd's, ASTM)
3. Hire them as subcontractor
4. They provide "pathway" (not standardized)
5. Step-by-step validation using accredited labs
6. Part-by-part certification (no template reuse!)

**Why Oil & Gas Avoids AM:**
> "Oil and gas companies don't do mass production. One or 5 parts and they don't need to enter the certification process so they prefer to be more on the conventional side. **Even if AM could give them lots of opportunities, that certification alone causes lots of issues** so they don't like that process."

**Hypothesis NH.7 Update:** ‚ùå Was "Certification is A barrier" ‚Üí ‚úÖ **"Certification is THE barrier"**

### ‚úÖ **STRUCTURAL vs NON-STRUCTURAL FRAMEWORK (CRITICAL PIVOT)**

**Status:** **ADOPTION PATHWAY VALIDATED**

**Dr. Nasiri's Advice:**
> "**Focusing too much of your time on AM of structural parts could be challenging and time consuming. It would be better to focus on non-structural and less risky.** The part should be in the phase of **non-critical and non-simple manufacturing** section to be more acceptable."

**Definitions:**

**Structural Part:**
- Under loading and movement
- Higher risk of replacement
- Under a lot of stress
- Failure = operational chaos
- **Certification required** (costly, time-demanding)

**Non-Structural Part:**
- Not load-bearing
- Failure doesn't stop operations
- Easier to certify OR no certification needed
- **This is your entry point**

**Sweet Spot:** "Non-critical AND non-simple manufacturing"
- Complex enough that AM adds value (not just machining a block)
- Non-critical enough that certification is manageable

**Examples from Other Interviews:**
- Hopkins: Wire conduit trays (polymer, non-structural)
- Mark Kirby: Obsolete pump components (15-year-old parts)
- Atlantic Excel: Before-Christmas part delivery (urgent, lower risk)

## üí∞ COST vs LEAD TIME TRADE-OFF

**Dr. Nasiri:**
> "AM part cost most of the time is higher than the normal one yet, **the lead time is lower. We have to find the balance for each sector.** For sure the part would be higher in cost but we can claim that **we can deal with complexity better than the normal conditions.**"

**Framework for When AM Wins:**

| Scenario | Traditional Mfg | AM | Winner |
|----------|----------------|----|----|
| **Simple part, high volume** | Low cost, fast (tooling amortized) | High cost | Traditional |
| **Simple part, low volume (1-5)** | Medium cost, medium lead time | High cost, low lead time | Depends (urgency?) |
| **Complex part, high volume** | Tooling expensive but amortized | High per-unit cost | Traditional (eventually) |
| **Complex part, low volume** | Tooling + machining very expensive | High cost but no tooling | **AM WINS** |
| **Obsolete part (no supplier)** | Impossible or months lead time | High cost, days-weeks | **AM WINS** |
| **Urgent replacement (downtime = $1M/day)** | Weeks | Days | **AM WINS** |

**Your Platform's Role:**
- Input: Part complexity, volume, urgency, downtime cost
- Output: "Traditional vs AM" recommendation with ROI calculation
- **This is H50/H54 business case calculator!**
