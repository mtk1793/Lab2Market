## CORE INTERVIEW QUESTIONS (5 Main + 2 Side Questions)

### MAIN QUESTION 1: What Is the Most Expensive Systemic Pain Across the Portfolio?
**[Combines: Global Perspective + Recurring Pattern + Strategic Priority]**

> **"Michal, at your level you see patterns across the full Kinross network, not just one mine. Across your portfolio, what is the single most expensive maintenance problem that repeats across sites and consistently limits performance? I'm asking for the issue that drives both operational drag and executive concern."**

> **Listen for direction:**
> - **A) Knowledge silos:** lessons are discovered locally but not transferred fast enough
> - **B) Measurement inconsistency:** site-to-site comparisons are weak or non-comparable
> - **C) Cost-performance tension:** OPEX pressure without confidence in reliability trade-offs
> - **D) Enterprise risk visibility:** insufficient foresight on high-impact failure exposure
> - **E) Competitive performance gap:** concern about outperforming peer miners on reliability

> **Follow-up probe:** "If you could remove only one of those constraints in the next 12 months, which one changes Kinross results the most, and why?"

**Why This Matters:** Anchors his true portfolio-level pain and strategic focus.

### MAIN QUESTION 2: Which Operating Philosophy Wins - Standardize or Federate?
**[Combines: Authority Model + Strategic Trade-Off + Change Mechanics]**

> **"Assume you have an 18-month mandate and a meaningful budget to improve global maintenance ROI. Which philosophy is more realistic and higher-impact at Kinross in practice, not in theory?"**

> **OPTION A - Centralized Standardization**
> - Common data architecture and consistent KPI definitions
> - Standard playbooks from top-performing sites propagated network-wide
> - Faster consistency, but heavier change-management and local resistance risk

> **OPTION B - Federated Learning Network**
> - Strong cross-site peer exchange with local adaptation freedom
> - Faster local buy-in, but slower standardization and uneven execution quality
> - Greater dependence on leadership discipline and governance cadence

> **Decision probe:** "Which model aligns with your actual authority, political reality, and speed requirement over the next 2 planning cycles?"

**Why This Matters:** Exposes his real change strategy and constraint boundary.

---

### MAIN QUESTION 3: How Fast Does Knowledge Move from One Failure to Portfolio Action?
**[Combines: Knowledge Transfer Velocity + Data/Analytics Maturity]**

> **"Take a critical failure scenario at one site. Walk me through the full chain from incident to portfolio action: detection, diagnosis, communication, adaptation, and verification. Where does the delay usually happen, and which delay is most costly?"**

> **Specific probes:**
> - "How quickly do other sites receive actionable guidance?"
> - "How often does guidance convert into changed field behavior?"
> - "What percent of similar failures are avoided because transfer happened in time?"
> - "How much of your current process is data-driven versus experience-driven?"

> **Bottom-line probe:** "Is transfer velocity a top strategic bottleneck, or an accepted operating reality in a distributed mining portfolio?"

**Why This Matters:** Tests whether the dominant issue is knowledge flow, data quality, or execution governance.

### MAIN QUESTION 4: If You Had a Dedicated $10M, Where Does It Go First?
**[Combines: Investment Priority + ROI Logic + Execution Practicality]**

> **"If you had a protected $10M annual envelope for maintenance transformation, what is the first investment you would make to generate measurable value within 12-18 months, and why does that beat the alternatives?"**

> **Choice framework:**
> - A) Data and analytics foundation (integration, visibility, predictive signal quality)
> - B) Portfolio knowledge platform (capture, codification, rapid transfer)
> - C) Standardization of one high-impact maintenance process across sites
> - D) Specialist capability deployment (cross-site reliability experts)
> - E) Targeted technology acceleration (sensing, AI-assisted diagnostics)
> - F) Another option with stronger business case

> **Execution probes:**
> - "What KPI moves first?"
> - "What leading indicator appears within 90 days?"
> - "What condition would tell you to reallocate or stop?"

**Why This Matters:** Translates strategic language into real capital-allocation behavior.

### MAIN QUESTION 5: What Is the Real Adoption Sequence Inside Kinross?
**[Combines: Governance + Sponsorship + Scale Path]**

> **"Assume a pilot proves value at one site. Walk me through the real adoption sequence to scale across the network: who sponsors, who challenges, what evidence is mandatory, and what rollout shape is most feasible under current governance?"**

> **Adoption probes:**
> - "Do you mandate rollout, or build consensus with staged expansion?"

> **Decision probe:** "If you wanted this live at scale in 24 months, what would have to be true by quarter 2?"

**Why This Matters:** Clarifies whether this is a local optimization play or enterprise-scale opportunity.
