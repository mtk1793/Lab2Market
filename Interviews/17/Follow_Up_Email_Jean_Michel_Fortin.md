## Key Insights: Why Mining is Different

Your explanation of the **fundamental mismatch** between AM capabilities and mining needs was a crucial market segmentation lesson:

**Volume Economics:**
> "It's the volume... we're big, big mines. It takes a lot of material to make it happen."

- Mining needs: **Thousands** of identical wear parts (conveyor rollers, crusher liners, etc.)
- AM capability: **Tens** of custom parts
- **Result:** Economics don't work at mining scale

**Seasonal Constraints:**
> "1.5 to 4 months operating season... must order 12 months in advance"

- Arctic seasonality = planning problem (not manufacturing problem)
- Even with digital inventory + AM, still need perfect 12-month forecast
- **Result:** AM doesn't solve the core constraint

**Supplier Certification Model:**
- Parts come OEM-certified with equipment
- Zero certification burden on Agnico
- AM would **shift burden** to mine (adds complexity you don't want)

## The ONE Opportunity: Breakdown Insurance

Your insight was critical:

> "Predictive maintenance—we know it right now. It's easy to order the right parts. It's the **breakdowns** that are the problem. That's where your idea of 3D printing—when it breaks and it's not supposed to break, or when an accident happened. What do you do? That's where the real downtime happens."

This distinction between:
- ✅ **Predictive maintenance** = SOLVED (sensors, data, forecasting working)
- ❌ **Reactive breakdowns** = UNSOLVED (unpredictable failures, emergency response)

...is exactly the use case where AM adds value. But you're right that mining won't be the early adopter:

> "I'll be happy if the technologies evolved enough. Might be a good idea if it works."

**Translation:** Prove it in other industries first, then we'll consider.

## What I'm Doing with This Information

**1. Market Segmentation Refinement**

Your interview clarified which industries are **good fits** vs. **poor fits**:

**✅ GOOD FIT:**
- Military/Defense (low-volume, high-mix, sovereignty)
- Offshore Oil & Gas (remote, low-volume, certification in-house)
- Aviation/Space (low-volume, design iteration, obsolescence)

**❌ POOR FIT:**
- Large-scale mining (high-volume, commodity, supplier-certified)
- Mass manufacturing
- Any industry where volume >> customization

**2. Deprioritizing Mining Outreach**

Based on your feedback, I'm **not** pursuing other large mining companies aggressively. Instead:

Your distinction is now how I segment use cases:

- Industries with **solved predictive maintenance** → AM = breakdown insurance only
