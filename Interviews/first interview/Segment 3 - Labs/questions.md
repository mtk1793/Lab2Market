## Testing & Certification Business Model Insights

### Unique Characteristics:

**Regulatory Environment:**
- Compliance-driven demand (customers need certification to sell/operate)
- Standards and procedures defined externally (ISO, industry standards)
- Documentation and traceability critical
- Quality management systems required

**Service Delivery:**
- Variable project timelines (simple tests vs. complex validation)
- Equipment-intensive but also knowledge-intensive
- Results may trigger additional work (re-tests, failure analysis)
- Customer education often required

**Revenue Model:**
- Project-based billing (per test/certification)
- Mix of standard tests and custom validation
- Some recurring revenue from customers with regular needs
- Premium pricing for rush services

### Operational Challenges:

**Demand Variability:**
- Customer project timelines drive urgency
- Seasonal patterns in some industries
- New product launches create spikes
- Regulatory changes drive certification waves

**Quality Requirements:**
- Zero tolerance for errors in test execution
- Audit and accreditation pressure
- Documentation overhead for compliance
- Continuous training and competency validation

**Scalability Constraints:**
- Equipment capacity limits
- Specialized expertise availability
- Accreditation scope (what you're certified to test)
- Physical space for samples and testing
