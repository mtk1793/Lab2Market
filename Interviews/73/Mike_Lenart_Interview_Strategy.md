## CORE INTERVIEW QUESTIONS (5 Main + 2 Side Questions)

### MAIN QUESTION 1: What Is the #1 Maintenance Pain at Greenstone Right Now?

> **"Mike, running mill maintenance at Greenstone — which is a relatively new operation in a remote location — what is the single biggest operational headache you're dealing with right now? I'm talking about the thing that keeps showing up and threatening your availability numbers."**

> **Listen for:**
> - **A) Parts availability:** can't get the right part fast enough when something breaks
> - **B) Skilled trades shortage:** can't find or keep millwrights, HVAC techs, mechanics (consistent with his LinkedIn posts)
> - **C) Unplanned downtime:** specific failure modes recurring in the mill
> - **D) Remote location logistics:** everything takes longer and costs more to get to Geraldton
> - **E) New site ramp-up:** equipment still being optimized, lots of unexpected failures in year 1-2

> **Follow-up:** "When that problem hits, what's the first thing you do — and where does the current process break down?"

---

### MAIN QUESTION 2: How Do You Currently Handle Urgent Parts That Aren't in Stock?

> **"When a critical component fails and you don't have it in inventory — say a custom impeller, a hydraulic manifold, or a specialty gear component — what does your process look like to get it? And how often does that situation lead to extended downtime?"**

> **Listen for:**
> - OEM lead times (weeks? months?)
> - Whether he uses emergency freight, third-party machining, or field fabrication as workarounds
> - Estimated cost of downtime per hour or per day (gold mine = very high)
> - Whether he keeps an unusually large safety stock to compensate for lead time risk
> - Whether he has experience with 3D printing or knows of it being used elsewhere in mining

> **Follow-up:** "Has anyone ever brought you a 3D-printed replacement part as an option? What was your reaction?"

---

### MAIN QUESTION 3: How Do You Evaluate a New Maintenance Technology or Supplier?

> **"If a new supplier or technology came to you claiming it could reduce your parts lead time significantly — maybe produce a replacement component locally in days instead of weeks — what would it take for you to trust it enough to actually use it on a critical piece of equipment?"**

> **Listen for:**
> - Material certification and equivalency proof requirements
> - Trial/pilot expectations before full adoption
> - Need for OEM approval or internal engineering sign-off
> - Price sensitivity vs. downtime cost trade-off
> - Whether he needs a champion upstream (VP Ops, corporate reliability team) to approve new approaches

---

### MAIN QUESTION 4: What Does Your Current Spare Parts Inventory Strategy Look Like?

> **"Running a remote mine site, how do you currently manage critical spare parts? Do you carry a large physical inventory on-site, rely on fast freight from suppliers, or some combination? And where does that strategy fall short?"**

> **Listen for:**
> - Capital tied up in physical inventory (carrying cost pain)
> - Parts that are obsolete or hard to source due to equipment age
> - Whether inventory management software/systems are effective or lacking
> - Whether there's a corporate standard vs. site-level discretion

---

### MAIN QUESTION 5: If You Could Change One Thing About Parts & Maintenance Supply Chain, What Would It Be?

> **"If you had a magic wand and could fix one thing about the way spare parts and maintenance supply chain works in mining — what would you change?"**

> **Listen for:**
> - Speed of delivery to remote sites
> - Traceability and certainty about part quality
> - Ability to get custom/legacy parts without waiting for OEM
> - Better visibility into what suppliers have available and when
> - Reducing dependence on a single OEM for critical components
