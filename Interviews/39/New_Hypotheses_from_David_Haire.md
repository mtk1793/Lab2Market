## New Hypothesis #2: Metal AM ROI Model Requires Service Provider, Not Manufacturer-Owned (NH.51)

**Hypothesis:** Metal additive manufacturing equipment is too expensive for most manufacturers to own. The viable business model is: **manufacturers buy parts from centralized service providers (like Atlantic XL), not manufacture themselves**. Your platform connects buyers to these service providers.

**Evidence:**
- David (repeated): "When it's so expensive to buy the machinery, how do I get my money back?"
- "You're almost cheaper just to say, here's somebody make me this part, I'll buy it off you"
- Titanium example: "You'd have to do a lot of putting parts in humans to get your money back on titanium machinery"
- Machine shop model is how manufacturers get specialty parts

**Type:** Revenue/Business Model
**Related to:** VP.4 (Qualified Client Pipeline for facilities), VP.14 (Pricing transparency)

**Implication:**
- Do NOT target manufacturers as equipment buyers
- DO target manufacturers as parts buyers + service providers (like Atlantic XL) as sellers
- Service provider model (Atlantic XL) is proven; marketplace connecting buyers to them is needed

**Recommended Testing:**
- Interview 5 metal AM service providers (Atlantic XL, PolyUnity, others)
- Ask: "What's your customer acquisition cost? How do you find customers?"
- Ask: "Would you pay commission for qualified customer leads?"
- Validate the commission-based marketplace model

**If Validated:**
- Focus platform heavily on the supply-side (filling Atlantic XL's pipeline)
- Less focus on manufacturer self-sufficiency, more on external sourcing
- Revenue model: 10-15% commission is viable (David didn't disagree with this)

## New Hypothesis #3: IP/Design Sharing Resistance Requires Trust & Confidentiality (NH.52)

**Hypothesis:** Manufacturers have strong IP concerns about sharing designs and specifications. A successful platform must explicitly address **privacy, security, and confidentiality** to overcome this barrier. "Confidential marketplace" positioning (not open-source) is required.

**Evidence:**
- David: "Many companies will say, 'I own the specifications and drawings, I don't want anybody to share'"
- Contrast: "When money starts changing hands, it's a different world than downloadable files"
- Open-source models (Thingiverse, Prusa) only work for freely shared designs

**Type:** Platform Design / Trust & Safety
**Related to:** VP.13 (Multi-role collaboration workspaces), VP.8 (Secure design file sharing)

**Recommended Design Features:**
- Role-based access control (only right people see specs)
- Audit trails for who accessed what
- NDA integration at platform level
- Separate "confidential project" vs. "public design" zones

**Recommended Testing:**
- Show 3 CME manufacturers mockups of "confidential workspace" feature
- Ask: "Would you trust this platform with your designs?"
- Measure: Would the feature increase willingness to use the platform?

**If Validated:**
- Position as "Confidential Procurement Platform for Advanced Manufacturing"
- Lead with security/trust, not openness
- Maybe even white-label for large manufacturers (OEMs)
