## David's Expert Feedback (14:08 - 16:43)

**David's Response:**

**On the ecosystem/network:**
- Canada Makes is a network connector with a code showing:
  - Who has what machinery/capability/materials
  - But only reaches ~15% of Canadian market
  - **Gap opportunity**: Another 85% might need your solution

**On digital inventory:**
- Challenge: Intellectual property concerns
  - Many companies: "I own the specifications and drawings, I don't want anybody to share"
  - Contrast: Thingiverse/Prusa - public library of downloadable files (free designs)
  - **Key insight**: "When money starts changing hands, it's a different world"

**On business case (most critical insight):**
- **Challenge with business model**: Especially for metal AM
- Metal machinery is **expensive** - hard to make money back
- Manufacturing equation:
  - Cost of machinery + powder + cleanup + changeovers
  - Often cheaper to just buy parts from someone else

**Example given**: Titanium knees/hearts for humans
- Would need massive volume to pay back equipment investment
- Special materials (titanium) require expensive machinery

**David's concern**: "When it's so expensive to buy the machinery, how do I get my money back?"

**Bottom line**: "When you get into metal stuff, you're almost cheaper just to say 'here's somebody make me this part, I'll buy it off you.'"
