## INTERVIEW 68: AURANGZEB SIDDIQUI - ENTERPRISE DISTRIBUTION MODEL INSIGHTS

### Who & Why This Interview Matters
- **Role:** RVP - Operations at WESCO Distribution (~$22B revenue, Fortune 500)
- **Experience:** 17+ years in distribution, 2023 to present in leadership
- **Limitation:** He's operations, not procurement decision-maker (important boundary)

---

### 🔍 CRITICAL FINDING: ENTERPRISE DISTRIBUTORS DON'T SOLVE CUSTOM COMPONENT PROBLEMS

**The Core Model:**
- WESCO distributes; does NOT manufacture or develop custom products
- When customers need specialized/custom components: **"We do not develop any products for any specialized customer"**
- **Lab2Market Implication:** WESCO is NOT your buyer for a custom manufacturing platform

**How Custom Orders Actually Work:**
1. Customer requests specialty component
2. WESCO places special order with existing supplier (made-to-order)
3. Ships directly supplier → customer OR through WESCO if bundled
4. **Lead time:** Long (exact duration unclear, but "not a due to deploy in a week")

---

### 📋 Supplier Selection Criteria (CS.1 VALIDATED)

When WESCO evaluates a NEW supplier offering new technology:

**Primary Decision Factors:**
1. **Reputation in Market** (most important)
2. **Supply Chain Presence & Stability** (operational reliability)
3. **Product Acceptability** (market validation)

**What Does NOT Move the Needle:**
- Certifications/badges (not mentioned)
- Third-party verification (not mentioned)
- New supplier "discount" (they want established players)

**The Supplier Model:**
- One major supplier per product category (exclusive)
- Backup suppliers only for supply disruption risk
- Suppliers must come to them with proven track records
- WESCO does NOT champion new suppliers; suppliers must be established first

---

### 📊 Post-COVID Supply Chain Reality Check

**Tariff Impact (Surprisingly Low):**
- Only 1-4 products affected directly
- WESCO could pass costs to customers (pricing model)
- Did NOT force major localization or new supplier search

**Local Sourcing Pressure:**
- Acknowledged concern about China/Europe dependency
- But in practice, NO forcing function for new local suppliers yet
- This weakens the "post-COVID must-go-local" narrative

### 🔗 Sales & Channel Insights

**How to Reach Procurement Teams:**
- Cold outreach: 12-minute conversation, casual interest
- Warm intro through Aurangzeb: Much higher acceptance (he offered to facilitate)
- **Quote:** "If I reach out to them through you, they would be more accepting"

**Organizational Friction:**
- Procurement ≠ Operations ≠ Supplier Development ≠ Inventory Management
- Each function has separate incentives; they don't coordinate

**Implication for Sales Strategy:**
- WESCO is a CHANNEL to end customers, not a buyer
- End customers (utilities, contractors, industrial manufacturers) are where custom sourcing demand exists
- If something doesn't fit WESCO's exclusive supplier model, it falls through
