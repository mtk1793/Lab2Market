# Interview 10 Follow-Up: Jordan Cumming - Moskowitz Framework Questions

**Date:** March 2026 (Follow-up session)  
**Interviewee:** Jordan Cumming (Enginuity, Navy Tech/Ocean Operations)  
**Context:** Exploring the Moskowitz Framework and segmentation strategy applied to his operational reality

---

## PART 1: VALIDATE SEGMENT PLACEMENT (Are We in the Right Cluster?)

### Q1: The Morning Worry Question (Moskowitz's #1 Insight)

> **"When you wake up in the morning and think about your offshore operations, what problem worries you most? What keeps you up at night?"**

**Why This Matters:**
- This reveals his TRUE primary pain (not stated desires)
- Determines if he's truly Cluster 2 (Downtime Risk) or if he has elements of Cluster 1/3
- The Moskowitz insight: "The mind knows not what the tongue wants" — we need to observe, not ask directly

**What We Know:**
- Previous interview: "Offshore inventory tracking prevents emergency shutdowns"
- This suggests Cluster 2 (Downtime/Risk-Averse)
- But maybe his actual morning worry is different?

**Listen For:**
- Is the #1 worry: "We'll have a catastrophic failure and can't get the part" (Cluster 2)?
- Or is it: "We're carrying too much inventory offshore and it's expensive" (Cluster 1)?
- Or: "We can't prove to DNV/regulators which parts we used" (Cluster 3)?

---

### Q2: The Metric Question (What Makes You Look Good to Your Boss)

> **"What's the ONE operational metric that, if you improved it by 15-20%, would make you the hero in your organization? What would your boss celebrate?"**

**Why This Matters:**
- Reveals decision criteria
- Shows which KPI system he's measured against
- Different clusters optimize for different metrics:
  - **Cluster 1:** Cost reduction (15% inventory carrying costs)
  - **Cluster 2:** Reliability/uptime (99.5% parts availability)
  - **Cluster 3:** Compliance certification (100% documented parts genealogy)
  - **Cluster 4:** Predictive efficiency (AI-driven maintenance scheduling)

**Listen For:**
- Does he talk about cost? (Cluster 1)
- Does he talk about availability/emergency response? (Cluster 2)
- Does he talk about certification/documentation? (Cluster 3)
- Does he talk about predictive maintenance? (Cluster 4)

---

### Q3: The Catastrophic Failure Scenario

> **"Tell me about the worst-case scenario on an offshore platform. What would a critical failure look like? And when that moment happens, what's your nightmare — is it the 'we don't have the part' problem, or is it the 'we have to prove to regulators that we sourced it legally' problem, or is it 'we spent money on parts we never used'?"**

**Why This Matters:**
- Tests which pain point drives his decision-making
- Not asking what he wants, but what he's actually trying to avoid
- Different clusters have different nightmare scenarios:
  - **Cluster 2 (Downtime Risk):** "$10M/day downtime because we couldn't get the part in time"
  - **Cluster 3 (Compliance):** "Regulators shut us down because we can't prove parts genealogy"
  - **Cluster 1 (Cost):** "We spent $5M on emergency inventory we never used"

**Listen For:**
- Emotional intensity around failure mode
- Which failure scenario he brings up first
- What metric he assigns as cost/impact

---

## PART 2: HORIZONTAL SEGMENTATION (Not Hierarchy)

### Q4: The "Chunk vs. Thin Sauce" Question

> **"I want to ask you two hypothetical solutions. Neither is 'better' — they're just different. Tell me which one solves YOUR problem better:**

> **Option A:** "We ensure your critical offshore parts are always available within 24-48 hours. No more waiting. Pre-qualified suppliers, certified facilities. You focus on operations; we handle availability."

> **Option B:** "We analyze your historical part usage patterns and predict which parts you should stock, which you should order on-demand, and which you should print locally. We automatically optimize which to buy vs. print to minimize your total inventory cost."

> **Which one makes you think 'YES, that's exactly what I need' — and why?"**

**Why This Matters:**
- Tests whether he's Cluster 2 (Risk/Downtime) or Cluster 1 (Optimization/Cost)
- Both solutions are valid; we need to know which is RIGHT for him
- Moskowitz insight: Don't try to convince chunk-lovers to like thin sauce; build for their preferences

**What This Reveals:**
- **If he picks Option A enthusiastically:** Cluster 2 (Risk-averse). You're in Downtime/Risk-Averse segment. Build for availability, not cost.
- **If he picks Option B enthusiastically:** Cluster 1 (Optimization). You're in AI-Optimization segment. Build for efficiency, not speed.
- **If he says "both":** He hasn't articulated his true primary pain yet. Dig deeper with Q5.

---

### Q5: The Trade-Off Question

> **"Now imagine you had to choose ONE. Budget constraints. You can have either:**

> **Perfect availability (guaranteed 24-48 hour turnaround, but your total inventory costs you $500K/year)**

> **OR**

> **Perfect optimization (AI-driven decisions cut your inventory spend to $300K/year, but 72-120 hour wait times for some parts)**

> **Which one keeps you employed? Which one gets you fired?"**

**Why This Matters:**
- Forces prioritization
- Shows actual risk tolerance
- Moskowitz: Different customers prioritize differently; don't impose your priority on them

**What This Reveals:**
- **If he chooses Perfect Availability:** Definitely Cluster 2. Risk mitigation is primary.
- **If he chooses Perfect Optimization:** Definitely Cluster 1. Cost efficiency is primary.
- **If he agonizes:** Possible hybrid segment; might need to ask more contextual questions.

---

## PART 3: REJECT THE "PLATONIC" SOLUTION

### Q6: The "I Want Everything" Check

> **"If you had unlimited budget and resources, would you want a platform that does EVERYTHING — digital inventory, AM coordination, compliance tracking, cost optimization, predictive maintenance, all integrated?"**

**Expected Answer:** "Yes, of course" (irrational answer)

**Follow-up (The Real Question):**

> **"Now be realistic. If we built a mega-platform with all those features, and you had to spend 40 hours learning it, dealing with integration issues, managing 3 different vendor contracts... would you actually use it? Or would you rather have ONE thing that solves your primary pain really well, even if it doesn't do everything?"**

**Why This Matters:**
- Moskowitz principle: "We weren't making an error; we were doing ourselves a massive disservice"
- Reveals whether he wants an integrated platform or a focused solution
- Clusters want different things:
  - **Cluster 2 (Risk):** Wants focused solution (availability) + simple integration
  - **Cluster 1 (Cost):** Wants focused AI engine (don't care about compliance tracking)
  - **Cluster 3 (Compliance):** Wants focused cert automation (don't care about AM routing)

**Listen For:**
- Does he express frustration with "bloated software"?
- Does he say "I just need X, everything else is noise"?
- Does he worry about integration complexity?

---

### Q7: The MVP Prioritization Question

> **"If we could only build ONE of these three things for you in the next 6 months, which would have the most impact on your operations:"**

> **A) Pre-qualified offshore printer network + digital inventory for critical parts (guaranteed 24-48 hr delivery)**

> **B) AI system that analyzes your consumption patterns and tells you what to stock vs. print vs. order (cost optimization)**

> **C) Automated certification tracking dashboard that proves to regulators which parts you sourced from where (compliance proof)**

> **Which one would you fund first?"**

**Why This Matters:**
- Forces real prioritization
- Determines which MVP to build for him first
- Shows true segment placement

**What This Reveals:**
- **MVP A = Cluster 2 (Risk)**
- **MVP B = Cluster 1 (Optimization)**
- **MVP C = Cluster 3 (Compliance)**

## PART 4: DEEP DIVE ON HIS SEGMENT

### Q8: If Cluster 2 (Downtime/Risk) — The Availability Deep Dive

> **"You mentioned offshore inventory tracking prevents emergency shutdowns. Walk me through the scenario:**

> **- How often do you actually face a critical parts shortage offshore?"**
> **- What's the current lead time when that happens?"**
> **- What does the emergency response process look like (helicopter, next boat, local fabrication)?"**
> **- What's the total cost of an emergency parts delivery?"**
> **- Would you pay that cost weekly to guarantee 24-48 hour availability instead?"**

**Why This Matters:**
- Quantifies the downtime value
- Determines willingness to pay for Cluster 2 solution
- Tests whether risk mitigation is truly primary pain

---

### Q9: If Cluster 1 (Optimization) — The Cost Deep Dive

> **"You mentioned challenges with inventory efficiency. Walk me through:**

> **- How much are you currently spending on parts you carry but never use?"**
> **- What percentage of that inventory could theoretically be sourced on-demand (AM or rapid delivery)?"**
> **- What's your tolerance for wait time if it meant 30% inventory cost reduction?"**
> **- Who specifically would get the credit for inventory optimization savings — you or procurement?"**

**Why This Matters:**
- Quantifies cost optimization value
- Determines willingness to accept (modest) longer lead times
- Shows if cost metric is actually primary

---

### Q10: If Cluster 3 (Compliance) — The Certification Deep Dive

> **"You mentioned regulators and certification. Walk me through:**

> **- How much time do you currently spend on compliance documentation when sourcing parts?"**
> **- What's your biggest regulatory bottleneck — is it DNV approval? Lloyd's review? Local authority sign-off?"**
> **- How often do regulators push back on your parts sourcing?"**
> **- If we automated certification tracking so you had documentable proof instantly, would that unlock new supply sources?"**

**Why This Matters:**
- Tests if compliance is actually primary pain or secondary
- Determines willingness to use offshore/non-traditional suppliers if certified

---

## PART 5: MOSKOWITZ'S FINAL TEST (Happiness Metric)

### Q11: The "Deliriously Happy" Question

> **"Imagine we solve your primary pain (whichever one it is from earlier questions). You're getting:**

> **- [If Cluster 2] Instant 24-48 hour parts availability offshore, guaranteed**
> **- [If Cluster 1] 20% inventory cost reduction through AI-driven sourcing decisions**
> **- [If Cluster 3] Instant certification proof for any supplier you want to use**

> **How much happier would your operations be? And more importantly — would you recommend this to other offshore operators you know?"**

**Why This Matters:**
- Moskowitz's insight: "Difference between 60 and 78 [happiness] = difference between wincing and being deliriously happy"
- Tests whether focused solution > bloated platform
- Indicates willingness to be champion/reference customer

- Determines go-to-market channel
- Shows decision-making authority
- Reveals if you should position through industry contacts (Thales, Cove) or direct

## INTERVIEW FLOW RECOMMENDATION

1. **Q1-Q3** (20 min): Validate segment placement through morning worry, metrics, and catastrophic scenarios

3. **Q6-Q7** (10 min): Reject platonic solution; force MVP prioritization
4. **Q8-Q10** (15 min): Deep dive on HIS specific segment
5. **Q11** (5 min): Happiness/recommendation metric
6. **Q12-Q13** (10 min): Channel, go-to-market, and ecosystem fit
