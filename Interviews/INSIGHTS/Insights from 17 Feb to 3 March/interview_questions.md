# Interview Questions: Joy Romero — Executive Advisor Innovation, CNRL
**Interviewee:** Joy Romero  
**Position:** Executive Advisor Innovation, Canadian Natural Resources Limited (CNRL)  
**Focus Areas:** Data strategy & AI/ML, technology innovation, supply chain excellence  
**Interview Purpose:** Understand CNRL's data-driven approach to operations, technology evaluation methodology, and how they think about innovation at scale; identify opportunities for partnership

---

## Q1: CNRL's Data & Predictive Strategy — Scale & Competitive Advantage

**Key Question:**
You mentioned CNRL has been using neural networks for 20+ years to predict facility inputs (ore composition) and set plant conditions ahead of time. Walk me through that architecture — how do clean data, predictive models, and real-time operations integration create competitive advantage? And what gives CNRL confidence that these predictions are accurate enough to automate plant settings without human intervention?

**Sub-questions:**
- At what cadence do you update predictions? (hourly, daily, per facility type?)
- How many data points feed into a typical prediction model? (millions, billions?)
- What's the cost of a failed prediction? (product loss, facility damage, safety risk?)
- Are there facility types or conditions where predictions are weaker? (extreme environments, new asset types?)
- How do you handle model drift over time? (retraining frequency, performance monitoring?)
- Across CNRL's global operations (Canada, West Africa, offshore), do you run the same models or customize by region?
- What's the threshold of data quality required before a model is "production-ready"? (accuracy %, data completeness %?)

## Q2: Building AI/ML Capability Across the Organization — Scaling Expertise

**Key Question:**
You mentioned that 7–8 years ago, CNRL intentionally built AI/programming capacity by training domain experts (engineers, geologists) to code. Walk me through that program — why invest in this instead of hiring data scientists? And how did you overcome the adoption barriers to get hundreds of people using these tools?

**Sub-questions:**
- What was the ROI on training domain experts to code? (reduced time to insight, better models, faster adoption?)
- How many employees have gone through the programming courses now? (hundreds across divisions?)
- What's the structure of those courses? (internal, external providers, accredited?)
- Are there types of problems where domain-expert-coded solutions outperform hired data scientist solutions?
- How do you prevent knowledge silos? (e.g., if one person becomes the "AI expert" in a business unit, what happens if they leave?)
- What's the failure rate? (% of people who take the course but don't apply it on the job?)
- How does CNRL celebrate and publicize AI successes? (you mentioned weekly stories on intranet?) What's the strategy behind public internal communications?
- For a company trying to replicate this approach, what's the minimum critical mass to sustain momentum?

## Q3: Data Quality as Competitive Moat — Lean Six Sigma & Integration

**Key Question:**
You emphasized that CNRL's data is exceptionally clean because of Lean Six Sigma discipline, and that clean data is why AI models respond so quickly and accurately. But you also mentioned CNRL has acquired many companies and integrated their databases. Walk me through the process of standardizing and cleaning integration data at scale — and what would happen to an AI model if you suddenly had dirtier data flowing in?

**Sub-questions:**
- What percentage of CNRL's data comes from legacy acquisitions vs. organic systems? (e.g., 80% legacy, 20% organic?) 
- How long does it typically take to integrate and clean a newly acquired company's data? (months, years?)
- What's your data governance structure across business units? (centralized, decentralized, hybrid?)
- Do you have a standing "data quality team," or is it embedded in business units?
- If a new facility or sensor system came online with noisier data, how would you handle it? (quarantine it, clean it, or let models learn to tolerate it?)
- What happens to model accuracy if data quality drops? (e.g., "10% increase in noise = X% decrease in prediction accuracy"?) 
- Are there facility types or geographies where data quality is lower? (e.g., West Africa vs. Canada?)
- How do data quality standards compare between operational data (equipment sensors) vs. transaction data (inventory, procurement)?

## Q7: Operational Resilience & Risk Management — Role of Data/AI

**Key Question:**
You mentioned CNRL had zero production downtime from unavailable spare parts. But at scale—200+ facilities, legacy acquisition data, regional variations—how do you prevent cascading failures? What's the risk management framework that ensures a neural network prediction doesn't catastrophically fail at a critical facility?

**Sub-questions:**
- What's the process for validating a new predictive model before deploying it to critical facilities? (How much historical data? How many predictions tested?)
- Have you ever had a model "surprise" you with an incorrect prediction that could have caused safety/downtime issues? (How did you recover?)
- How do you handle edge cases—facilities or conditions the model has never seen before?
- What's the rollback procedure if a model starts degrading?
- Do you keep human backup systems running in parallel with AI predictions? (I.e., when a neural network predicts "order part X," do humans still double-check?)
- How do you communicate uncertainty in model predictions? (E.g., "80% confident" vs. "99% confident"?)
- What's the governance structure for AI risk? (Who approves deployment to safety-critical systems?)
- How do you ensure that data quality standards don't slip over time as systems age and change?
