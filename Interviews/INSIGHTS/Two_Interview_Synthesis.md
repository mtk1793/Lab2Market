## Key Learnings from Two Interviews

### Learning #1: One Great Interview > Five Mediocre Ones

**Bassey provided:**
- Coverage of ALL 5 segments
- 18 strongly validated hypotheses
- 8 new hypotheses discovered
- 3 critical pivots identified

**Khashayar provided:**
- Deep dive into 1 segment (Labs)
- 3 hypotheses validated
- 3 new hypotheses discovered
- Confirmed NDA challenge

**Implication:**
Prioritize interview QUALITY over quantity. One 2-hour comprehensive interview yields more insights than five 30-minute surface-level chats.

---

### Learning #2: Different Segments Require Different Approaches

**Industry (Bassey):**
- More open, willing to share details
- Can quantify pains ("3-4 weeks", "tariffs", etc.)
- Responds well to structured questions

**Academia (Khashayar):**
- NDA-restricted, general information only
- Qualitative pains ("expensive", "immature", etc.)
- Better suited for process/operational questions

**Implication:**
Tailor interview scripts by segment. Don't use same questions for labs vs. manufacturers.

---

### Learning #3: Triangulation Increases Confidence

**Example: Communication Pain**
- Validated in Interview #1 across 5 segments
- Re-validated in Interview #2 (lab reporting challenges)
- Now very high confidence (‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê)

**Example: Market Maturity**
- Interview #1: "We have OEM partners"
- Interview #2: "Decades till replacement"
- Triangulation reveals: Hybrid adopted now, full replacement decades away

**Implication:**
Keep asking same questions across interviews to build triangulation and increase confidence.
