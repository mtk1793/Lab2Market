## ‚è∞ **SPARE PARTS PAIN POINT: 6-MONTH WAIT FOR CRITICAL VALVE**

### **The Crisis Scenario:**

**Mahmoud Asked:**
> "If you could change one thing about OMV's spare parts process to make it faster, cheaper, or better, what would it be?"

**Chris's Answer:**

> "Ideally, this spare part is available when you need it. Whenever the valve breaks and I need to wait half a year to get a new one, because it's a special whatever valve... this is a no-go. Sometimes, I mean, I say it's really an extreme case maybe, but it can happen."

### **The Problem Breakdown:**

**Scenario:**
1. ‚öôÔ∏è **Critical component fails:** Specialized valve (custom/obsolete)
2. üö´ **Not in stock:** Not a common spare (too expensive to stock all variants)
3. üìû **Order from OEM:** Lead time = **6 months**
4. ‚è∏Ô∏è **Production stopped:** Equipment down while waiting
5. üí∞ **Lost production:** Revenue loss every day

**Why 6 Months?**

**Specialized Valve = Custom Manufacturing:**
- Not mass-produced (low volume)
- Requires tooling, machining, testing
- OEM production queue
- Offshore-grade certification (DNV, API)
- Shipping, customs, installation

**Financial Impact:**

> "This is a no-go. It's reducing your downtime and your lost production. Bring your downtime to the minimum."

**Calculation (Rough):**
- Offshore platform production: ~10,000-50,000 barrels/day
- Oil price: ~$80/barrel
- **Daily revenue:** $800K - $4M
- **6-month downtime:** $144M - $720M lost revenue

**Even if:**
- Workarounds exist (bypass valve, use backup)
- Production reduced (not stopped completely)
- **Still:** Multi-million dollar problem

### **The Root Cause (Chris's Insight):**

> "From all the spare parts, what do we really have on stock? And what do you make to stock, and where is the flexibility?"

**Inventory Dilemma:**

**Stock Everything:**
- ‚úÖ Always available
- ‚ùå Massive working capital tied up
- ‚ùå Parts obsolete before used

**Stock Nothing (JIT):**
- ‚úÖ Low working capital
- ‚ùå Long lead times (6 months)
- ‚ùå Production risk

**Current State (OMV):**
- Historical approach: Stock common parts
- **Problem:** Special parts (valves, pumps) = long lead time

**What's Needed:**
- Intelligent stock optimization
- Flexibility for custom/special parts
- **AI + AM = solution**
