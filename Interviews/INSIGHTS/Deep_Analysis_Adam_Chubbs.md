### 1. Spare Parts Criticality & Redundancy Strategy üîÑ

#### What Adam Said:
> "Being in remote location, remote prime power diesel, that means perch, perch criticality is is is, is **critical for lacking better term**, I guess. Um, usually what happens, um you know, for a major failure. Typically we've got **redundancy built in, like, it's an N+ 1 or N+ two**, uh, generation line up."

> "For example, some of the remote diamond mines, some of the remote gold mines in the north, they have an **N+ 2**. So if you look at your generation, they'll have enough sparing, um, available. So there's, there's always one unit available on standby, and there's usually always one out on maintenance, right, plus whatever line required."

#### Critical Insights:
1. **Redundancy levels by industry**:
   - **Utilities (transmission)**: N+1 (two transmission lines, one carries peak)
   - **Utilities (substations)**: Breaker-and-a-half configuration
   - **Mining (remote diamond/gold)**: **N+2** (production downtime is everything)
   - **Offshore**: Similar redundancy requirements

2. **Spare parts strategy**:
   - **System redundancy**: Built-in backup units (warm standby)
   - **Critical spares**: Standardized across fleet (e.g., one breaker type fits multiple sites)
   - **5% rule**: Transformers - "always 5% of whatever we have in service that, that's in stock"
   - **Storm spares**: Additional inventory for weather-related failures
   - **Vendor alliances**: Consignment stock with OEMs (Caterpillar, Detroit Diesel)

3. **Distribution vs. transmission**:
   - **Distribution**: "Failure maintenance realm" - don't inspect until it fails
   - **Transmission**: Proactive maintenance, contingency planning

#### Quantitative Data:
- **Mining redundancy**: N+2 (3 units: 1 running, 1 standby, 1 maintenance)
- **Transformer spares**: **5% of fleet** in stock
- **Standardization**: "Within the whole asset fleet, there's usually one 1 critical spare that we could deploy anywhere within the system"

#### Strategic Implications:
- ‚úÖ **Validates remote operations pain**: N+2 redundancy is EXPENSIVE - they're desperate to avoid downtime
- ‚úÖ **Validates standardization opportunity**: Utilities standardize breakers/transformers across fleet - AM parts must also be standardized
- üÜï **Vendor consignment model**: OEMs stock parts on consignment - could AM providers do the same?
- üÜï **Mining is the highest-pain customer**: N+2 redundancy = highest willingness to pay for faster parts
