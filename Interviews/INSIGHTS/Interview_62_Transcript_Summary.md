# Interview 62: Joy Romero — CNR Transcript Summary

**Date:** March 3, 2026  
**Interviewee:** Joy Romero, Executive Advisor Innovation  
**Company:** Canadian Natural Resources Limited (CNRL) — Canada's largest oil & gas producer  
**Duration:** ~37 minutes (recorded)

---

## Key Insights

### 1. **CNRL Has Already Solved the "Spare Parts Crisis"**
- Joy explicitly stated: **"I'm actually not aware of any production that is being held up because of parts not available."**
- This is true across **all regions (BC, Alberta, Saskatchewan, Manitoba, offshore, West Africa)**
- CNRL has invested 20+ years in data-driven supply chain systems
- They use predictive reorder points, understand time-to-failure for all equipment, and run orders through automated systems
- When Joy worked at BP, they had the spare parts problem; at CNRL, it's solved

**Implication for our platform:** The "downtime prevention" narrative doesn't resonate with CNRL. They're already best-in-class. Our value must pivot to **efficiency gains** or **new capabilities** (not problem-solving).

---

### 2. **Digital Inventory ≠ What Joy Thinks It Means**
- **CNRL's definition of "digital inventory"**: All inventories are digitized, warehouses use bots to pick parts, systems track everything
- **Our definition of "digital inventory"**: On-demand 3D printing instead of storing physical spare parts
- Joy initially thought we meant IT-ifying their systems (which they already have)
- **However**, once clarified, Joy saw the value: *"With smaller pieces, right? But not larger, not larger pieces. But with smaller smaller ones, yeah, there are people who loved that kind of stuff."*

**Implication:** We need clearer terminology. CNRL sees AM for smaller parts as useful, not as a replacement for heavy/large components.

---

### 3. **CNRL Uses AI/Neural Networks at Scale (20+ Years)**
- Joy mentioned they **were using neural networks 20 years ago** to model ore bodies and predict plant inputs
- They **set plant conditions ahead** of incoming ore, preventing upsets that cause product loss
- This is a **major operational advantage**: Most plants react to problems; CNRL predicts and prevents them
- They've built **internal AI capability** intentionally:
  - Created courses for domain experts (engineers, geologists) to learn programming (7–8 years ago)
  - Now have **hundreds of employees** across the company with AI/programming skills
  - People self-select into roles as "AI go-to" people in their business units
  - Success stories are featured weekly on internal comms

**Implication:** CNRL is extremely sophisticated on AI/ML. They're not looking for "AI solutions"; they're looking for **clean data and access to it**. They already have the talent and infrastructure.

---

### 4. **Data Quality is Their Competitive Edge**
- CNRL is **Lean Six Sigma certified** → their data is **clean by discipline**
- They have **millions and billions of data points** from:
  - Decades of operations across multiple geographies
  - Acquired companies' databases that they've consolidated
  - Internal systems (ERP, MRO, equipment sensors)
- **They don't need external data** — they have enough internal data to build AI models
- The clean data → faster AI feedback loops → faster identification of issues
- This drives **hundreds of millions of dollars in annual gains** from AI applications

**Implication:** For companies like CNRL, the bottleneck isn't algorithms or AI expertise; it's **clean, integrated data**. Our platform's value might be in **data orchestration and integration**, not just AM/logistics.

---

### 5. **Technology Evaluation is Formal & Structured**
- CNRL has a defined path: **www.cnrl.com/working-together** → two streams:
  1. **Commercial stream**: New commercial technology → goes through Supply Management → formal evaluation process
  2. **Experimental/Pilot stream**: Requires piloting or is experimental → goes through Technology stream
- All submissions go into a **database** (not someone's desk) for tracking and accountability
- Pilots come from **business units pulling** solutions (not corporate pushing)
- Each pilot has a **business case** associated before starting
- Success or failure leads to either **adoption plan** or **rejection**, with clear methodology
- **Website is the funnel** — this is how contractors/vendors should apply

**Implication:** To reach CNRL effectively, we must:
1. Go through the right channel (supply-chain platform = commercial stream)
2. Understand the evaluation criteria
3. Have a clear business case
4. Not push; let them pull based on demonstrated value

---

### 6. **CNRL Views AM (3D Printing) as Niche, Not Revolutionary**
- Some CNRL operations have 3D printers on-site
- Joy didn't express skepticism, but also not evangelism
- The interest is **limited to smaller parts** — not critical infrastructure
- They're evaluating it pragmatically: "What size, what other kind of thing"
- No urgency mentioned around AM adoption

**Implication:** For CNRL, AM is a **tool in the toolbox**, not a strategic imperative. Our platform must position AM as a **cost/efficiency play for non-critical spares**, not as transformational.

---

### 7. **The Platform Must Fit into CNRL's Ecosystem**
- CNRL has **existing systems**: SAP, MRO, inventory management, vendor portals
- They have **IT governance** and **data governance** (Lean Six Sigma discipline)
- New solutions must **integrate** with existing systems and respect **data security**
- They have an **AI team** focused specifically on external AI solutions
- They work with **networks in the digital/AI space** to understand what's out there
- Internal teams = domain experts with programming capacity (not separate AI specialists buying solutions)

**Implication:** A platform for CNRL must:
1. Be **vendor-agnostic** (integrate with their systems, not replace them)
2. **Add value to clean data**, not require data cleaning
3. **Respect their governance** and security policies
4. Show **quantified ROI** (hundreds of millions at scale)

---

### 8. **CNRL's View of External Innovation**
- Joy: *"I would never say that we have all the good ideas we don't."*
- They actively scan the market through their formal process
- But they're **pragmatic**: If internal solutions work, they don't need external
- They're **looking for dance partners, not vendors**
- They have capacity to **pilot, evaluate, and adopt** if there's clear value
- They're **not early adopters**, but they're not laggards either

**Implication:** We're pitching to a **sophisticated buyer** who:
- Won't be impressed by hype
- Wants quantified ROI
- Will evaluate rigorously
- Might move fast IF value is clear
- Will standardize IF it works across multiple units

---

### 9. **CNRL's Procurement Process Strength is Both Ally & Barrier**
- **Ally**: They have a process, we can follow it (not arbitrary decisions)
- **Barrier**: We need to get through the process, which takes time and rigor
- **Best path**: Work through the business units first (get them to "pull" the solution), then go through formal evaluation

**Implication:** To sell to CNRL, we should:
1. Find a **business unit sponsor** (someone who sees the problem and wants to solve it)
2. Run a **pilot** that generates internal success stories
3. **Then** submit through the formal www.cnrl.com process
4. Have **all documentation ready** (business case, evaluation criteria, ROI)

### Uncovered

**Tier-1 oil & gas operators (CNRL, Equinor, Shell, BP) have solved spare-parts availability through decades of investment in predictive systems and data-driven inventory management. The real pain point is not "do we have parts?" but "how do we certify additive-manufactured parts affordably at scale?"** Absence of certified AM supply chains is the competitive moat and regulatory barrier, not the logistics problem.

Supporting evidence: CNRL reports zero production downtime from parts unavailability across all facilities (Canada, West Africa, offshore) for 20+ years. Their competitive advantage is Lean Six Sigma data discipline (clean data → better predictions → faster decisions), not sophisticated algorithms. They use 20-year-old neural network models to predict facility inputs and optimize operations pre-emptively.

### Evidence

1. **Data Quality = Competitive Edge:** CNRL's 20-year investment in clean data infrastructure (Lean Six Sigma discipline across 200+ facilities) drives prediction accuracy more than algorithm sophistication. Hundreds of millions $/year in operational gains.

2. **Certification Is the Real Barrier:** Craig Dicken (ExxonMobil) articulated the core question: "How do you scale AM certification affordably?" This is why DRL 3 (Digital Readiness Level for AM) matters—not as a feature, but as the unlocking mechanism for cost amortization across customer base.

3. **Formal Procurement Beats Hype:** CNRL uses structured evaluation (www.cnrl.com/working-together) requiring business-unit pull, successful pilots, and formal RFP process. Vendors "pitching" struggle; vendors solving business-unit problems with data succeed.

4. **Operators Segment Clearly:** Best-in-class operators (CNRL, Equinor) solved spare-parts problem; mid-size independents and smaller operators *have* the acute pain. Strategy should target mid-size with proof-point from majors.

### Pattern

**Misalignment between startups' assumptions ("operators have supply chain pain") and operator reality ("we solved that; new pain is certification/integration").**

Market divides into:
- **Tier-1 Majors:** Data/AI-sophisticated, formal procurement, low acute pain → Reference partners, not customers
- **Mid-Size Operators:** Standard procurement, acute spare-parts/downtime pain, limited data maturity → Primary customer target
- **Small Operators:** Reactive procurement, willing to try pilots, no internal R&D → Secondary target

### Decision

Pivot customer targeting from "operators with spare-parts pain" (Tier-1 majors don't have this) to **"mid-size operators with certification/integration barriers."** Use CNRL as validation of best-in-class thinking, not as primary customer.

Reposition platform pitch from "digital inventory solves supply chain" to **"DRL 3 certification scaling unlocks affordable AM supply chain."** This resonates with both pragmatic operators and government funders (NRC-IRAP).
