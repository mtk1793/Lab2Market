## 3. PIVOT #1: CERTIFICATION IS MANAGEABLE â€” NOT A SINGLE WALL

### What We Used to Believe
Certification = a monolithic, multi-year barrier blocking every part.

### What Lloyd's Register Actually Told Us (Interview #42)

Adam Saxty (LR Lead AM Technologist, UK) mapped the full 7-step AM certification process:

1. **Design Review** â€” part suitability for application
2. **Material Specification** â€” custom spec per part (no universal AM material standards yet)
3. **Facility Qualification** â€” personnel, machines, CAD-to-machine translation (**done ONCE per facility**)
4. **Feedstock Qualification** â€” powder reuse tracking, batch consistency
5. **Part Qualification** â€” prototype build + destructive testing
6. **Production Certification** â€” test coupons built alongside actual parts
7. **Installation Approval** â€” documentation complete â†’ part certified â†’ installed

**Trevor Butler's game-changing clarification:**
> *"You don't have to go through all that qualification process every single time. That should, I would think, be a prerequisite to being included in a digital inventory."*

### The Revised Certification Model

| Action | Frequency | Who Does It |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Facility qualification | **ONCE** per facility | AddManuChain + partner facility (Atlantic XL) |
| Part design review + qualification | **ONCE** per part design | AddManuChain + LR |

| Installation approval | **Every order** | Operator |

> **Pivot:** The barrier is front-loaded, not recurring. Our pre-certified parts library model is **fully validated by Lloyd's Register themselves**.
