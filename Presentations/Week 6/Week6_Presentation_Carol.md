## SLIDE 1: The Problem We're Solving üí∞

### **What Problem Are You Solving?**
**Companies are bleeding 25-30% annually on "Insurance Stock" - spare parts that sit for 10+ years with ZERO inventory turns.**

**Example:**
- $10M in spare parts inventory
- 25% annual holding cost = **$2.5M/year** in:
  - Cost of capital (10%)
  - Storage/warehouse (6%)
  - Insurance (3%)
  - Obsolescence (8%)

"We'll free up $4M in working capital by converting Insurance Stock to digital files. Your CFO keeps that cash. You pay us 1-2% annual management fee."

### **üö® CRITICAL REALITY CHECK (Interview #28 - Andrew Smith):**

**Andrew Smith (30-year veteran, Kiewit/Jacobs/Pratt & Whitney):**
> "Offshore Newfoundland, they have **spares that they share**. Where they've got several rigs within 100 km of each other, there are critical spares that each one of the rigs have, **even though they're not the same companies**. Because it's a cost reduction - chances are that one spare is not gonna be needed on more than one rig at a time. So they can get the helicopter and move it over. They have an **equipment sharing agreement**. The delay getting out from St. John's can be significant (300 kilometers), but if you're within 50-100 kilometers out there, you're done."

**What This Means:**
- Oil & Gas operators **already coordinate spare parts** (informal digital inventory)
- Helicopter sharing is **faster than St. John's manufacturing** (2-hour flight vs 3-day AM)
- Multi-company agreements exist (they've solved trust/IP problem informally)
- **Our value must beat helicopter sharing** OR target parts **not suitable for sharing**

**Strategic Implication:**
Focus on **ultra-low-volume, high-complexity parts** (<50 units/year) that are:
1. Too large/heavy for helicopter
2. Require certification (can't share between different equipment models)
3. Obsolete (OEM no longer manufactures)

### **üÜï IP PROTECTION SOLUTION (Interview #27 - Keith Healey):**

**Keith Healey (Former ExxonMobil/Suncor R&D Advisor):**
> "OEM equipment suppliers see it as a **threat to a loss of business**. Oil companies are afraid of **legal consequences**. I think what needs to happen is additive manufacturers need to **come together in a consortium** and propose a secure digital warehouse and **negotiate a price on the IP for the blueprint**. The OEM has to see 'Where's the money for me?' They will want **commissions or payment not just for the license, but on everyone that is made**."

**Revenue Model for OEMs:**
1. **Upfront license fee** to OEM (one-time payment for IP rights)
2. **Per-print royalty** to OEM (ongoing revenue stream - e.g., 10% of part cost)
3. **Consortium structure** (multiple AM providers, not single company)

**Example:**
- OEM releases valve design (upfront fee: $50k)
- Every time valve is printed: OEM gets 10% royalty
- If 20 valves printed over 5 years at $5k each = $10k ongoing revenue to OEM
- **OEM makes MORE money** than selling 20 physical valves (no manufacturing cost)

### **üÜï LEAN/SIX SIGMA FRAMEWORK (Interview #28 - Andrew Smith):**

**Andrew Smith (Six Sigma Black Belt, CME Lean Facilitator):**
> "Physical inventory is waste - necessary as part of doing business, but not actually making you money. You would compare the waste between three models: (1) Stock, (2) Just-in-time delivery, (3) Additive manufacturing."

**Lean Metrics for CFO Pitch:**
- **Inventory Turns:** Current 0 turns ‚Üí Target 2-4 turns (digital on-demand)
- **Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO):** Current 3,650 days (10 years) ‚Üí Target 90 days
- **Cash-to-Cash Cycle:** Free up capital for growth investments
- **Waste Elimination:** Physical inventory = "necessary waste" ‚Üí Digital = zero waste

**Keith Healey on AI:**
> "AI has a huge opportunity for creating efficiencies. Oil and gas needs to **lean in heavily into AI** to get those efficiencies and to crack the emissions issue."

**Strategic Implication:**
- Frame digital inventory as **"Lean Supply Chain"** (waste elimination)
- Lead with **CFO-level ROI** (working capital), not technology
- Use AI for **optimization and prediction** (not just predictive maintenance)
