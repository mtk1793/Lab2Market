### **BIG DISCOVERY #2: Helicopter Sharing is the REAL Competitor**

**Andrew Smith (30-year veteran, Kiewit/Jacobs/Pratt & Whitney):**
> "Offshore Newfoundland, they have **spares that they share**. Where they've got several rigs within 100 km of each other, there are critical spares that each one of the rigs have, **even though they're not the same companies**. Because it's a cost reduction - chances are that one spare is not gonna be needed on more than one rig at a time. So they can get the helicopter and move it over. - Multi-company trust/IP agreements already exist
- **We must beat helicopter sharing OR target parts unsuitable for sharing**

**Andrew Smith (Six Sigma Black Belt, Lean Facilitator):**

### **BIG DISCOVERY #6: Consortium Model for OEM IP**

**Keith Healey:**
> "OEM equipment suppliers see it as a **threat to a loss of business**. Oil companies are afraid of **legal consequences**. I think what needs to happen is additive manufacturers need to **come together in a consortium** and propose a secure digital warehouse and **negotiate a price on the IP for the blueprint**. The OEM has to see 'Where's the money for me?' They will want **commissions or payment not just for the license, but on everyone that is made**."

**Revenue Model for OEMs:**
1. **Upfront license fee** to OEM (one-time payment for IP rights)
2. **Per-print royalty** to OEM (ongoing revenue stream - e.g., 10% of part cost)
3. **Consortium structure** (multiple AM providers, not single company)

**Example:**
- OEM releases valve design (upfront fee: $50k)
- Every time valve is printed: OEM gets 10% royalty
- If 20 valves printed over 5 years at $5k each = $10k ongoing revenue to OEM
- **OEM makes MORE money** than selling 20 physical valves (no manufacturing cost)
