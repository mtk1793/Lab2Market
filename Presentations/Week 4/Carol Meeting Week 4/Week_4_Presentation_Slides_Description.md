### **THE FINDING:**

**We learned that certification‚Äînot business case, not technology‚Äîis THE primary barrier to AM adoption in oil & gas and defense sectors** (Segment: Industry operators + academic experts, n=4)

**Evidence:**
- **Dr. Ali Nasiri** (Dalhousie, oil & gas expert): *"AM developed so fast that authorities couldn't catch up. Even if AM could give them lots of opportunities, that certification alone causes lots of issues so they don't like that process."*
  
- **Jordan Cumming** (Enginuity, ocean tech): On temporary AM parts: *"Is it certified against marine grade corrosion? Because it's technically not from a known source, there's also a liability issue. It's a bit of a gray area."*

**Pattern:**
All 4 industry-focused interviews (Nasiri, Kirby, Cum ming, Power) mentioned certification BEFORE cost or business case. Regulators (DNV, Lloyd's Register, ASTM) developing standards but "too general, not component-specific."

**Contradiction:**
Aerospace has solved this (Tronosjet certified laser part = can reproduce without re-certifying). But oil & gas/maritime won't invest in certification for 1-5 parts/year (not mass production).

**Surprise:**
The barrier isn't "is AM technically viable?" (everyone agrees it works). It's "can we certify it fast enough to justify 1-off production?" This flips our assumption that business case was #1 barrier.
