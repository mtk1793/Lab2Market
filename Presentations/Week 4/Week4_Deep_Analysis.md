## ðŸ” The 4-Question Interview Framework

### Mastering Customer Discovery

![Four Key Interview Questions](/Users/admin/.gemini/antigravity/brain/e9119f86-4f66-4b5b-88b6-68c56f76065c/four_key_interview_questions_1769738627757.png)

#### Question 1: "Tell me about the last time this happened"
**Purpose:** Get concrete stories, not hypothetical opinions

**Why it works:**
- Forces specificity (real experience vs. abstract thinking)
- Reveals actual behavior (not stated preferences)
- Uncovers context you wouldn't think to ask about

**Example Application:**
- âŒ "Do you struggle to find 3D printing suppliers?"
- âœ… "Tell me about the last time you needed to source a 3D printed part"

---

#### Question 2: "How did you solve this problem?"
**Purpose:** Discover existing workarounds (proof of demand)

**Why it works:**
- If they're already solving it (even badly), the pain is REAL
- Workarounds show willingness to invest effort
- Reveals your competition (current solution, not other startups)

**Critical Insight:** 
> Your competition is NOT other AM marketplaces.  
> Your competition is manual Google searches + phone calls + existing supplier relationships.

---

#### Question 3: "What was frustrating about the way you handled it?"
**Purpose:** Identify pain points in current solution

**Why it works:**
- Reveals what customers actually care about (not what you assume)
- Prioritizes features by lived experience
- Surfaces emotional pain (stronger buying signal than rational need)

**For AddManuChain:**
- If they say "It took too long" â†’ Emphasize speed
- If they say "I couldn't trust the quality" â†’ Emphasize certification
- If they say "I had too many options" â†’ Emphasize precision matching (H43!)

---

#### Question 4: "How much did this solution cost?"
**Purpose:** Reveal willingness to pay

**Why it works:**
- **If they paid before, they'll pay again**
- Establishes budget range
- Differentiates "nice to have" from "must have"

**Value Hierarchy:**
- **Paid in cash** = High value, proven willingness to pay
- **Paid in time/effort** = Medium value, shows importance but unclear pricing
- **Free workaround** = Low value, may not be monetizable

## âŒâœ… Wrong vs. Right Questions

### The Art of Non-Leading Questions

![Wrong vs Right Questions](/Users/admin/.gemini/antigravity/brain/e9119f86-4f66-4b5b-88b6-68c56f76065c/wrong_vs_right_questions_1769738648809.png)

### Why "Would you use X?" is a Terrible Question

**The Problem:**
1. **Lead the witness:** Suggests what answer you want
2. **Hypothetical:** People are bad at predicting future behavior
3. **Binary:** Loses nuance and context
4. **Confirmation bias:** You hear what you want to hear

**Example from Week 4:**

| âŒ Wrong Question | âœ… Better Questions |
|------------------|---------------------|
| "Would you use a marine robot for data collection?" | "How do you currently collect oceanic data?" |
| | "What's the most frustrating part of the process?" |
| | "Have you ever tried a different way? What happened?" |
| | "If you could magically fix one thing about your process, what would it be?" |

### Application to AddManuChain Interviews

**Instead of asking:**
- âŒ "Would you use an online marketplace to find 3D printing facilities?"

**Ask:**
- âœ… "Tell me about the last time you needed a custom part made"
- âœ… "How did you find the manufacturer?"
- âœ… "What made that process difficult?"
- âœ… "If you could change one thing about finding suppliers, what would it be?"

## ðŸ”„ Hypothesis Testing Cycle

###The Continuous Learning Loop

![Hypothesis Testing Cycle](/Users/admin/.gemini/antigravity/brain/e9119f86-4f66-4b5b-88b6-68c56f76065c/hypothesis_testing_cycle_1769738729977.png)

### Four Stages of Scientific Customer Discovery

#### Stage 1: Form Hypothesis ðŸ’¡
**What:** Make a testable assumption about customer pain/behavior

**Example:**
- H39: "Real-time communication platform reduces project delays by 30-50%"

#### Stage 2: Conduct Interview ðŸ’¬
**What:** Ask questions that could **disprove** your hypothesis

**Critical Mindset:** Seek to be proven WRONG, not right

#### Stage 3: Analyze Results ðŸ”
**What:** Objectively assess evidence for/against hypothesis

**Framework:**
- **Strong support:** Customer described exact pain, paid to solve it before
- **Moderate support:** Customer acknowledged pain but hasn't paid to solve
- **Weak support:** Customer mentioned it but low priority
- **Rejected:** Customer doesn't experience this pain

#### Stage 4: Refine or Pivot ðŸ”„
**What:** Update hypothesis based on evidence

**Three Outcomes:**
1. **Validate:** Evidence strongly supports â†’ Move to implementation
2. **Refine:** Partial support â†’ Adjust hypothesis, test again
3. **Pivot:** Evidence contradicts â†’ Abandon, form new hypothesis
