## Slide 5: KEY LEARNING #1 â€” PAUL BLEMER INSIGHTS (Interview #41)

### Background:
Paul Blemer = AM industry expert with decades of experience in aerospace, defense, and high-stakes manufacturing adoption

### Critical Insight: **Why AM Adoption Has Stalled in Critical Industries**

| Challenge | Reality | Implication for AddManuChain |
|-----------|---------|------------------------------|
| **Certification Complexity** | Current system requires 2+ years per part; no standardized pathway for AM parts in defense/aerospace | Must "own the certification layer" â€” partner with C-CORE, build pre-certified library |
| **Cultural Barriers** | Risk-averse industries (O&G, defense, aerospace) fear AM = unproven = liability | Position as "breakdown insurance" not "innovation" â€” emphasize reliability + sovereign resilience |
| **Volume Economics** | Traditional manufacturing = economies of scale; AM = low volume premium pricing | Target customers with **N+2 redundancy** (Arctic utilities), **obsolete parts** (15+ year install base), **one-off custom** (Kraken Robotics AUVs) |
| **FAT Testing Gap** | Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT) for AM parts is undefined â€” no playbook | C-CORE partnership = FAT testing capability for marine certification |
| **AI/ML Adoption Lag** | ML for defect detection exists but is 5-10 years from full adoption due to trust issues | Position AI as "predictive maintenance + inventory optimization" not "quality control replacement" |

### ðŸ’¡ Strategic Pivot:
> **We must build a platform that handles certification complexity FOR the customer, not just provide digital files and hope they figure it out.**
