# Customer Discovery Interview Questions by Segment
## Lab2Market 3D Printing Ecosystem Platform

---

## üéØ Lab2Market Principle: You're Here to LEARN, Not to Pitch

**DO NOT mention your platform/solution during these interviews.**  
Your job is to understand their world, listen to their problems, and let them tell you what's broken.

---

## Interview Structure (30 minutes)

1. **Opening** (5 min) - Understand their world
2. **Pain Discovery** (10 min) - Uncover problems
3. **Current Solutions** (8 min) - How they handle it today
4. **Willingness to Change** (5 min) - Budget signals, past attempts
5. **Closing** (2 min) - Referrals, thank you

---

## Segment 1: Clients (Manufacturers/Buyers)

### Opening - Understanding Their World

1. "Walk me through how you currently source parts for [prototyping/production/spare parts]."
2. "What does a typical sourcing cycle look like for you, from need identification to delivery?"
3. "Tell me about the last time you needed a custom part made. What happened?"

### Pain Discovery

4. "What are the biggest challenges you face when sourcing 3D printed parts?"
5. "When was the last time something went wrong with a supplier? Walk me through that situation."
6. "What keeps you up at night about your supply chain for [custom/prototyped/additive] parts?"
7. "How do you currently find new 3D printing suppliers?"
8. "What's the most frustrating part of working with 3D printing facilities?"
9. "Tell me about a time when you had to reject parts from a supplier. What happened?"

### Certification & Compliance

10. "How do you verify that parts meet your quality/regulatory requirements?"
11. "What certifications do you typically require from suppliers?"
12. "Tell me about your last audit or compliance check. How did that go?"
13. "What's the hardest part about ensuring compliance with 3D printed parts?"

### Geography & Shipping

14. "Where are your current 3D printing suppliers located?"
15. "How much do you spend on shipping for these parts annually?" (If they know)
16. "Have you ever had issues with long lead times due to shipping? Tell me about that."
17. "Would you prefer working with local suppliers if you could find them? Why or why not?"

### Current Solutions

18. "How do you currently find and vet 3D printing facilities?"
19. "What tools or platforms do you use today for sourcing?"
20. "What do you wish was different about your current approach?"
21. "What's the biggest limitation of [system/process they mentioned]?"

### Willingness to Change

22. "Have you tried to solve this problem before? What happened?"
23. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about sourcing 3D printed parts, what would it be?"
24. "Who else in your organization is affected by these issues?"
25. "If you wanted to change your sourcing process, who would need to approve it?"
26. "What would 'better' look like for you?"

## Segment 2: 3D Printing Facilities (Supply Side)

### Opening - Understanding Their Business

1. "Walk me through a typical month in your business. How many jobs do you typically run?"
2. "How do most of your new customers find you?"
3. "Tell me about your best client. How did that relationship start?"

### Pain Discovery - Capacity & Revenue

4. "What percentage of your machine capacity is typically utilized?"
5. "When was the last time you had machines sitting idle? What happened?"
6. "What's the biggest challenge you face in finding new clients?"
7. "How much does it cost you to acquire a new customer?" (Marketing, sales time, etc.)
8. "What keeps you up at night about your business?"

### Certification & Credibility

9. "What certifications do you currently have?"
10. "How do potential clients verify your certifications?"
11. "Have you ever lost a deal because you couldn't prove your credentials? Tell me about that."
12. "What's the hardest part about getting certified for new standards?"

### Customer Acquisition

13. "Walk me through your sales process. How long does it typically take to close a new client?"
14. "Where do you spend most of your marketing/sales effort?"
15. "What's the most frustrating part of finding new business?"
16. "How do you differentiate yourself from competitors?"

### Current Solutions

17. "Do you currently use any platforms or marketplaces to find clients?" (If yes: "What do you like/dislike about them?")
18. "How do you currently showcase your work to potential clients?"
19. "What would make it easier for you to get more qualified leads?"

### Willingness to Change

20. "Have you tried other ways to get customers? What happened?"
21. "If you could fill 20% more of your idle capacity, how much additional revenue would that generate?"
22. "Would you be willing to pay a commission to get qualified leads? What % would be reasonable?"
23. "What would need to be true for you to try a new way of finding clients?"

## Segment 3: Labs (Testing & Certification)

### Opening - Understanding Their Workflow

1. "Walk me through your typical certification process, from request to completion."
2. "How many certification requests do you handle in a typical month?"
3. "Tell me about your busiest time. What does that look like?"

### Pain Discovery - Process Bottlenecks

4. "What's the most time-consuming part of your certification workflow?"
5. "Where do things typically get stuck or delayed?"
6. "What keeps you up at night about your operations?"
7. "Tell me about the last time something went wrong. What happened?"

### Administrative Burden

8. "How do you currently track incoming certification requests?"
9. "How much of your team's time is spent on administrative tasks vs. actual testing?"
10. "What's the most frustrating part of the paperwork/documentation process?"
11. "How do clients currently submit requests to you?"

### Visibility & Planning

12. "Do you have visibility into your request pipeline for the next 30/60/90 days?"
13. "How do you plan resource allocation (staff, equipment) based on upcoming work?"
14. "Have you ever been caught off-guard by a rush of requests? Tell me about that."

### Current Solutions

15. "What tools or systems do you use to manage your workflow today?"
16. "What do you wish was different about your current process?"
17. "If you could automate one part of your workflow, what would it be?"

### Willingness to Change

18. "Have you looked at digital workflow tools before? What happened?"
19. "If you could reduce administrative time by 50%, what would you do with that time?"
20. "What would a 'better' certification process look like?"
21. "Would you be interested in being listed on a platform if it brought you more business?"

## Segment 4: OEMs (Equipment Manufacturers)

### Opening - Understanding Their Manufacturing Strategy

1. "Walk me through how you currently manufacture [product line]."
2. "Tell me about your production footprint. Where do you manufacture today?"
3. "Have you explored distributed or contract manufacturing? What's your experience been?"

### Pain Discovery - Distributed Manufacturing

4. "What's holding you back from scaling production right now?"
5. "Have you considered using 3D printing for distributed manufacturing? What are your concerns?"
6. "What keeps you up at night about manufacturing scalability?"
7. "Tell me about a time when you couldn't meet demand. What happened?"

### Quality Control & IP Protection

8. "How do you ensure quality when working with contract manufacturers or suppliers?"
9. "What's your biggest concern about working with 3rd-party manufacturers?"
10. "Have you ever had issues with IP protection? Tell me about that."
11. "How do you currently monitor quality across multiple manufacturing sites?"

### Standardization

12. "Do you have standardized specs for parts that could be 3D printed?"
13. "How do you communicate design requirements to contract manufacturers?"
14. "What's the hardest part about maintaining consistency across suppliers?"

### Current Solutions

15. "What tools or systems do you use to manage distributed manufacturing today?" (If applicable)
16. "How do you currently share design files with manufacturers?"
17. "What would make you more comfortable with distributed 3D printing?"

### Willingness to Change

18. "If you could enable local/regional manufacturing while maintaining control, would that be valuable? Why?"
19. "What would need to be in place for you to trust a distributed 3D printing network?"
20. "Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"

## üìä What to Track After Each Interview

For EVERY interview, record:

1. **Pain Intensity** (1-10): How strongly do they feel the problem?
2. **Problem (Verbatim)**: Use THEIR exact words
3. **Current Solution**: What they do today (even if manual)
4. **Emotional Language**: Did they use words like "nightmare," "terrified," "frustrating"?
5. **Frequency**: How often does this problem occur?
6. **Budget Signals**: Did they mention existing spend or willingness to pay?
7. **Referrals**: Who else should you talk to?

---

## üö´ What NOT to Do

1. **Don't pitch your solution** - Even if they ask, redirect: "I'm still in learning mode. Tell me more about how you handle this today."
2. **Don't lead them to your solution** - Avoid: "Would you pay for a platform that..." 
3. **Don't ask hypotheticals too early** - Focus on their actual experiences first
4. **Don't mention other segments** - Each interview should feel unique to their world

---

## ‚úÖ Interview Success Metrics

**Good interview signs:**
- They use emotional language unprompted
- They quantify the problem ("We waste $X" or "It takes Y weeks")
- They ask when you'll have a solution (shows real pain)
- They offer to introduce you to others
- They go over the scheduled time (engaged)

**Red flags:**
- Vague answers ("It's not that bad")
- Can't quantify impact
- Haven't tried to solve it before (low motivation)
- Immediately ask about your solution (not feeling real pain)

---

## üéÅ Closing Questions (Last 2 Minutes)

Ask EVERY interviewee:

1. "Who else should I talk to who has similar challenges?" (Get 2-3 referrals)
2. "Would you be open to a follow-up conversation in a few weeks once I've learned more?"
3. "Is there anything I didn't ask that I should have?"

---

**Remember**: The best interviews are where you talk 20% and they talk 80%. Your job is to listen and learn, not to convince or pitch.
