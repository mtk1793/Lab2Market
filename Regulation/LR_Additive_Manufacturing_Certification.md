## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: When is a part suitable for additive manufacturing?
A part is suitable for additive manufacturing when it provides clear benefits over traditional methods, such as:
- Enabling complex designs
- Customisation or combining multiple components into one
- Potential for topology optimisation
- Shorter lead times
- Lower shipping and tooling costs
- Improved material efficiency

The decision should also consider:
- Material properties
- Part size
- Production volume
- Effects on downstream processes (coating, joining, inspection, maintenance)

Manufacturers must also ensure the AM process meets all regulatory, application, and service-life requirements.

---

### Q2: What is the difference between Classification and Certification for AM parts?

| | **Classification** | **Certification** |
|--|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Applies to** | AM parts essential to a classed vessel or marine equipment | Parts in marine or offshore applications that do NOT require classification |
| **Requirements** | Full compliance with LR Rules and Regulations | Must meet industry standards, project specifications, or OEM requirements |
| **Includes** | Design appraisal, LR-certified feedstock, facility qualification, LR surveys during manufacturing and testing | Design appraisal, facility and feedstock verification, inspection or testing |
| **LR Rules** | Full compliance required | Does NOT require full compliance with LR Rules for classed assets |

### Q4: What does the certification process involve?
The certification process follows these steps:

1. **Design Review** â€” Confirm compliance with standards and suitability for intended use
2. **Facility Verification** â€” Verify the AM facility including equipment, quality systems, and personnel; check feedstock conformity
3. **Part Qualification** â€” Approval builds and testing under LR survey to ensure material and mechanical properties meet specifications
4. **Certificate Issuance** â€” LR issues a certificate after successful inspection and testing, supported by complete documentation

> **Important:** Facility and feedstock certifications are valid for **three years** and require periodic audits and requalification.

- Process and part qualification by witnessing approval builds and testing
- Guidance on operator training and competency
- Technology qualification for new processes
- Ongoing support through periodic audits, requalification, and continuous improvement reviews
